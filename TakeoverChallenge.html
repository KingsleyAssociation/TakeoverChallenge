Real Capacity

Planning Your Human Load

Events don't break because of money; they break when there aren't enough people, or the wrong people, in the wrong spot at the wrong time. Before you build, work through this with your crew:

🔢How Many People, Total?

Count every job that needs a body: setup, door, food, cleanup, floaters. Add them up before you assume "we'll figure it out."

🕒What Time Do They Actually Work?

Setup happens hours before doors open. Cleanup happens after everyone else goes home. The same 3 people can't cover all three shifts well.

🔁Is Anyone Double-Booked?

If the same name is on setup AND door AND cleanup, that's not a plan — that's a single point of failure. Spread the load or bring in more people.

🔄Do You Have Backups?

People get tired, bail, or need a break. If one role has zero backup, name a second person now, not when they walk out at 1am.

Real-World Municipal Permits

02. The Compliance Desk (Do It Right)

Working inside the system is part of leadership. Secure real permits below to neutralize the risks tied to them.

Good To Know: The 2-Phase Event Permit

The City of Pittsburgh Office of Film and Event Management (OFEM) runs a two-phase portal on eproval.pittsburghpa.gov:

  • Phase 1: ROS (Reservation of Space): Submit up to 180 days early. Locks your venue spot for $25.
  • Phase 2: Full Application: Due 14 business days before setup. Needs a site map, security plan, and $1M liability insurance.
Good To Know: Real Pittsburgh Rules

Gathering in East End neighborhoods like Garfield, Homewood, East Liberty, and Larimer has real parameters worth knowing.

  • Schenley & East End Parks: Events over 25 people need a permit. No staking tents in the grass — use sand weights.
  • Block Parties: Limited to residential blocks, free entry, and can't block PRT bus lines without an approved reroute.
